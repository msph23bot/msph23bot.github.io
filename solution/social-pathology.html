Solution: Social Pathology
Answer: DEEP END

Written by Jonah Ostroff

Each puzzle in the Sociology department uses parts of the profiles of two fish. This puzzle uses the grid of swimmers from Hammerhead’s Instaclam page, and seven clips from Tuna’s Codify page.

Hammerhead’s page features a picture of a grid containing region boundaries, swimmer emojis, a start and goal, and some highlighted spaces. The text says to follow Haisu rules, but haisu normally contains numeric clues, and this picture doesn’t have any.

The grid contains seven regions, and seven song clips on Tuna’s page contain lyrics “nth time” for some n. Since haisu clues tell on which trip to a region the path must visit the clue, it makes sense to interpret these as the missing haisu clues. But which one goes where?

The text also says that “humans always hang out in an area that perfectly fits them”. The haisu regions have sizes 5, 6, 9, 10, 12, 13, and 15, which are exactly the lengths of the names of the artists for these songs:

Once the clues are assigned to swimmers, the haisu has a unique solution.
