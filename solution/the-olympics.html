Solution: The Olympics
Answer: BEAU

Written by Jenna Himawan

This is a minimeta-matching puzzle. There are 40 answers and 5 minimetas; each answer is used exactly once, and every minimeta takes 8 answers. The solutions to each minimeta are outlined below.

Blue Ring - Biathlon

Biathlon competitors need to be able to complete two events, although they overlap a little.

This minimeta revolves around words that can be made of two other words. However, one letter is shared between the words (it is the last letter of the first word and first letter of the second).

The answers that feed into the minimeta start with letters A-H, and are presented in order as follows:
ARCANE
BARELY
CONDONES
DIVESTING
ERASURE
FIBRED
GRANDMASTER
HEROES

The word splits are as follows:
ARC + CANE = ARCANE
BAR + RELY = BARELY
CONDO + ONES = CONDONES
DIVES + STING = DIVESTING
ERAS + SURE = ERASURE
FIB + BRED = FIBRED
GRANDMA + ASTER = GRANDMASTER
HER + ROES = HEROES

By reading the points of overlap, we receive the subpuzzle answer CROSSBAR.

Green Ring - Weightlifting

Weightlifting competitors need to find their weights.

In this subpuzzle, we look for units of weight that are contained inside the answers. The answers used, and the units of weight they contain, are as follows:

These highlighted letters spell the subpuzzle answer NUMERALS.

Bringing it all together

In order to extract a final answer from these subpuzzles, we use the pattern of the Olympic rings. We are told which answer corresponds to which ring, so we just need to write the letters on the rings in such a way that the same letter occurs at points of overlap.

There is one unique way (including if you allow answers to be written counterclockwise) to do so:

Reading the letters of intersection in "reading order" (top row first, left to right), we get the cluephrase SOLN BEAU, and our final answer, BEAU!

Author’s Notes

Initially, this puzzle included a Golf minimeta, which used the NATO phonetic alphabet. We decided this was uninteresting and replaced it.

Biathlon was initially called dressage, and the flavortext clued at "an article of clothing and an herb" (DRESS + SAGE = DRES(S)AGE). After some discussion, we decided on the current version of the flavortext.
