Solution: Caesar Studies
Answer: GOOFY

Written by Jonah Ostroff

This puzzle consists of 48 nonsense strings in alphabetical order. The first step is to decipher them. They’re all Caesar ciphers, so this isn’t too hard, although a few of them might not make much sense yet.

Five of the deciphered strings form a set of instructions, beginning with “SPLIT STRINGS INTO GROUPS” and “SOLVE EACH GROUP’S MINIPUZZLE”. So the first step is to do that: group the other 43 strings into five minipuzzles, and solve them.

The solutions to each minipuzzle are described below. They are presented in final solution order for reading convenience, even though there is no immediately-apparent way to determine the order until they are solved.

Minipuzzle 1

Ten of the decrypted strings are elements of ordered lists. They've been shifted by distinct amounts from 1 to 10, giving an order. Move each element forward in its list by the shift amount to get a new element. The first letters of the new elements spell SEMICIRCLE.

Nine strings are statements, most of which describe a word, but they're contradictory! And one of the statements (“Q IS A VOWEL”) is false. If you shift Q by 24 (like its statement), it becomes O, which is a vowel! Similarly, each statement is describing the word after it has been shifted. The only word satisfying these constraints is MAFIA.

Final Extraction

The remaining instructions say how to extract: “SHIFT MINIPUZZLE ANSWERS APPROPRIATELY”, “FIND SECRETS AFTER SHIFTING”, and “USE SECRETS AS INDICES”. The instructions use five different shifts, and when you encrypt a minipuzzle answer with one of these five shifts, it contains a number.

The instructions only make sense in a particular order, so the minipuzzle answers can be sorted in that same order. Index into the shifted answers using the revealed numbers to spell the final answer: GOOFY.
